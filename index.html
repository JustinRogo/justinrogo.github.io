About

A little about me

I work at the intersection of information, technology, data, and design. I like untangling complicated processes, automating tedious work, and building useful tools that make things easier to find and use. Outside of work, I’m usually experimenting with code, starting another side project, or tending to an entirely reasonable number of houseplants.

More about me

Professional focus

Building sustainable systems for library work

I’ve spent more than a decade working in academic libraries and have served the UConn School of Law Library since 2016. As Library Information Coordinator, I lead knowledge-management, reporting, automation, administrative, and cross-functional projects that turn operational needs into durable systems.

Selected work

Projects with practical impact

  • Knowledge management: Built a Confluence staff hub with an ever-growing collection of documentation, training resources, and institutional knowledge.
  • Data and reporting: Created Power BI dashboards for payroll, surveys, facilities, collections, scholarship, assessment, projects, and service feedback.
  • Workflow design: Led the study-room reservation transition from LibCal to EMS and built a Power Apps tool that automated daily statistics and checklist work.
  • Web and communications: Revamped the law library website and supported UConn Law Library Centennial communications and digital content.
